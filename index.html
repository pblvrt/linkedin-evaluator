The LinkedIn audit that tells you what to delete.

A senior-positioning-consultant-grade audit of your profile, scored across the 7 things that decide whether visitors become clients. Drag the bookmark, click on your profile, get the audit.

1. Drag this to your bookmarks bar

📊  Audit my LinkedIn

If your bookmarks bar is hidden: ⌘⇧B on Mac, Ctrl+Shift+B on Windows.

2. Run it on your profile

  1. Open your LinkedIn profile (linkedin.com/in/your-handle).
  2. Click the 📊 Audit my LinkedIn bookmark you just saved.
  3. The script scrolls your profile, expands every section, and copies an audit prompt to your clipboard. Then it opens Claude in a new tab.
  4. In Claude, hit ⌘V then enter. Audit appears.

What you get

A 0–100 overall score plus a weighted scorecard across the seven pillars: Headline · Visuals · About · Featured · Experience · Skills · Content. Three headline rewrites built on a real positioning formula. A new About opening. A Featured-section plan. A banner concept. One post idea for this week, grounded in your actual experience. Then it asks which buyer you want to attract and regenerates the rewrites for that buyer specifically.

Why this works

Most audits tell you to be authentic and tell your story. This one quotes your actual copy and tells you what to delete. The rubric is built around what makes a service-business profile generate inbound — not what makes HR happy.

How it works under the hood (for the curious)
  1. The bookmarklet is a tiny snippet that loads scrape.js from this site.
  2. scrape.js runs inside your LinkedIn tab — your already-logged-in session — and reads the rendered DOM. No server-side scraping, no LinkedIn ToS violation, no data sent anywhere.
  3. It builds a JSON snapshot of your profile and copies an audit prompt (with the snapshot inline) to your clipboard.
  4. Claude reads the prompt, fetches the audit framework from this repo, and produces the report in your own Claude session.
